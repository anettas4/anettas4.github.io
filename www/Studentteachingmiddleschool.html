    About Middle School

    At the middle school level, band consisted of two levels: the 6th Grade Concert Band as well as the 7th and 8th grade Honors Band. The 6th graders met once a week in the mornings for full band, and the 7th and 8th graders met twice a week for full band. Sectionals were held once a week for each instrument group and level. In addition, there were two levels of Jazz Band as well as a Middle School Beginning Band. I got experience teaching everything including full band, sectionals, and beginning band. My focus in middle school was to get students excited about band through using popular music. An example of a lesson I did can be seen below.

    Goal: Utilize Technology and Popular Music

    Concept Teaching Example:

    My students were having difficulty understanding dynamics so I used “From Now On” from the Greatest Showman. Using a program called Audacity, they were able to simultaneously see the dynamic range as well as hear it. The song in Audacity can be seen to the right.

    I then altered the dynamics to make the music get quieter which was also represented visually, as seen on the left. When we listened to the edited version- student responses included “no, no, no” and my favorite, “it feels like the end of the day on Christmas”

    We were working on a piece called “The Andromeda Connection” which had a part that went from forte (loud) to piano (soft). After talking about how the audacity graph should look, the students played and found that it looked nothing like they expected! They realized the differences they thought they were making in their playing weren’t coming across to the people listening to them. We played again with much better results!
