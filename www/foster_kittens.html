    Foster Cats

    So many purrs, so much love

    Link

    Link is my most recent foster kitten and also my toughest case! He came to me at less than a pound, needing to be hand-fed, and with a really bad eye infection and upper respitory infection. After round-the-clock feeding and attention, he started gaining weight. At his first vet visit, we learned that this little kitten who was the size of a three week old was actually about six weeks old! He never had a problem with his appetite and now is almost ten pounds. He had a single enucleation on his really bad eye. I fell in love with this pirate and adopted him!

    Xylo, Maestra, Rhapsody, Treble, and Forte

    This was my largest group of fosters that came to me from a hoarding situation. They arrived with upper respitory infections and a few had eye infections. One in particular, Xylo, was extremely nauseous from the antibiotics and refused to eat, so he had to be syringe fed around the clock. Everyone, including Xylo recovered well after a few months and were all adopted!

    Moon, Saki, and Sushi

    These three adventurous kitties came to me mainly for socialization. Being very scared of people, I helped socialize them by ensuring that they associated humans with good things- like food! That and the consistent influx of people that wanted to meet them and play with them helped them get adopted!

    Basie

    Not a kitten by any means, 8 year old Basie came to live with me for what was supposed to be 3 months, but it turned into 6 months! For the first 3 months, I spent time earning his trust, even though he was so terrified of me I couldn't look at him for too long without him running away. Suddenly, one day, he decided that I was alright and from that point on, demanded constant petting and attention.
