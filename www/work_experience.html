    Work Experience

    Shift Manager | Concerts at Bienen

    I have been working at the box office for Concerts at Bienen at Northwestern University since I was a freshman. In the box office as a Customer Service Representative, we answer customer questions and help customers place orders over the phone. Since my sophomore year, I have been a Shift Manager. I supervise my peers during day-to-day box office operations as well as setting up our box office before events and selling tickets day-of. In this position, I am the face of Concerts at Bienen Ticketing and manage patron issues.

    People's Music School

    In the summer of 2019, I got the chance to work at the summer program at People’s Music School. I got to work in many of the classes as an assistant and get experience working with children from first to 8th grade. When I wasn’t in a class, I was assisting with the digitization and organization of paperwork. We got to work with Third Coast Percussion on their piece “The Bell Ringer” which was a phenomenal experience. Some of the other programs included steel drumming, bucket drumming, and song writing

    Maine-Niles Association of Special Recreation

    For two summers I worked the summer camp with M-NASR as both an inclusion aid and working in a self-contained camp. I got the opportunity to work with and most importantly, connect, with children and adults with a wide variety of disabilities like physical disabilities, Autism, ADHD, Down Syndrome, and so many more. Making sure Music Education and education in general is accessible to any students with special needs is something I work tirelessly towards.

    Summer Tutor | District 63

    I knew I had an interest in education from the time I was a high schooler. I worked as a summer tutor for two years for District 63, assisting teachers in their classroom. This was probably one of the first experiences that really got me interested in education. One of the highlights of the experience was forming relationships with students and connecting with them.
