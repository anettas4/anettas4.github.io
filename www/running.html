    Running

    Life's a marathon, not a sprint

    I have run two marathons and enter the lottery for the Chicago Marathon every year to see if I'll get in. When people hear this, I imagine they assume I ran cross-country in high school and am very fit and very fast. None of that is true. I took up running as a way to stay healthy. Truth is, I'm really slow-but I don't really mind. For me, it is more about the training journey to a race, training to constantly improve yourself over your former self. Trying to take time off of every race. It is not about being the best at everything, it is simply about looking to improve yourself a little every day.

    I am currently training for a 10k as well as two 5ks leading up to it. I was not able to get into the lottery for the marathon this year so am focusing more on shorter races.
