    About Me

    A Peek Inside
    Home / About Me / Bio

    Music education can help spark a child's imagination or ignite a lifetime of passion. When you provide a child with new worlds to explore and challenges to tackle, the possibilities are endless. Music education should not be a privilege for a lucky few, it should be a part of every child's world of possiblity.

    Hillary Clinton

    I am a music educator passionate about creating an inclusive environment in my classroom. I think critically about my instruction and the kind of things I teach every day and am always looking to improve.

    I am a first-generation college student and American. These two things have greatly shaped who I am and how I teach.

    Although my first instrument was clarinet, I have taken classes or lessons in many other instruments. I enjoy practicing and performing Euphonium and Sousaphone particularly in marching band, pep band, or other informal settings.

    Why Me?

    I'm Organized

    At Northwestern, my course load was about double the average student. With up to 11 classes in a quarter, I learned how to stay organized and on task, even going as far as to plan my days down to the exact minute and bring this organization into my teaching, making sure everyone knows the agenda.

    I'm Hardworking

    I've been working since I turned 16 and was legally able to. I am no stranger to being in a workplace and work well with my co-workers and supervisors. I cannot wait to meet new collegues and start hearing all their great ideas and sharing some of my own.

    I Embrace Diversity

    My culture and where I grew up is a big part of my life and my teaching. I am always looking for ways to make my classroom the most inclusive and the most accessible. I welcome ideas from others on how to improve my classroom and my teaching.
